Introduction

This page provides extra materials for the paper "Distributed Resource Management for IoT-based Systems".

The explosive growth of Internet of Things (IoT), cloud data centers and network function virtualization services has raised the need for effective resource management for IoT. For emerging applications in blending IoT and cloud resources, resource management needs to be able to represent IoT devices, networks and cloud infrastructure at a high-level, while being able to link to diverse low-level types of IoT devices, network, and computing resources. Hence resource management in such a context demands a highly distributed modeling approach, which can be integrated and configured on the fly in order to facilitate resource provisioning and management. Although centralized resource management solutions exist for distributed systems such as cloud-based systems, very little has been done to address the question of distributed resource management for IoT. In this paper, we address this crucial research issue. We first present an information model for virtualized IoT resource modeling, which also incorporates software-defined gateways, network slicing and sub-networks. The key novelty of our information model is the representation of relationships among IoT resources, which is key to representing sets of IoT resources spanning across different sub-networks. Throughout our paper we illustrate our ideas using a realistic example from the emergency response domain, and we also present a proof of concept prototype.
